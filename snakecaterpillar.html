Caterpillar acts like a Snake



Caterpillar acting like a snake


By Admin
July 20, 2022


Do you think it's a snake? No, it’s a caterpillar in disguise.

This is a caterpillar of Common Mormon (Papilio polytes), common species of swallow-tail butterfly abundantly found in Western Ghats of India. These caterpillars show an interesting defence mechanism to ward off predators like birds, spiders or ants. They’re disguised as stubby snakes with a big green head with two fake eyes on either side of the head.

If they feel threatened, the caterpillars show the osmeterium which resembles the snake's red fleshy split tongue. This osmeterium can emit foul smell which also help to keep the predators away.

This osmeterium is unique to Swallow Tail butterflies.
